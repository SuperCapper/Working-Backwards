# Appendix B — Sample Narrative Tenets and FAQs

Companion material to [Chapter 4 (Communicating: Narratives and the Six-Pager)](../Part%20One/Being%20Amazonian/04-communicating.md). Expands on the optional "tenets" and "FAQ" sections mentioned there.

### Origin of Tenets

Former Amazon VP Dave Glick coined the practice after a series of unproductive narrative reviews with Bezos. Once a meeting finally converged on an agreed strategy, Bezos suggested writing the agreement down as a short list and putting it at the top of every future document on the topic — so each new review could "reload the cache" of prior decisions instead of re-litigating them. Bezos liked the pattern enough to push other teams to adopt it.

### What a Tenet Is For

A tenet resolves a recurring **tension between two legitimate goods** (e.g., speed vs. quality) by stating, in advance, which one wins when they conflict — so the trade-off doesn't have to be re-argued in every subsequent decision. Three real Amazon tenets illustrate the pattern:

- **"We don't make money when we sell things. We make money when we help customers make purchase decisions."** This resolved an early, genuinely controversial question — should Amazon host negative product reviews, since they can suppress a sale? The tenet reframes the business's purpose (enabling good decisions, not maximizing each transaction) so the answer becomes self-evident: negative reviews stay.
- **"When forced to choose between building something convenient for customers or convenient for ourselves, we'll choose the former."** Traced to a 1999 complaint email from a customer who needed help from her nephew to open Amazon's shipping packaging. The tenet directly produced a packaging redesign, and a decade later the company-wide **Frustration-Free Packaging** initiative.
- **"We don't let defects travel downstream... we'll invent and build systematic methods to eliminate that defect."** Illustrated with the fulfillment-center "switcheroo" problem (a package's actual weight not matching its expected contents) — solvable only with precise per-item weight data and reliable scales, not with individual apology emails. This tenet is credited as the direct ancestor of the customer-service "Andon Cord" (chapter 6) and was later folded directly into the **Insist on the Highest Standards** Leadership Principle.

### Sample FAQ Prompts

Beyond product-specific questions, the authors list general-purpose FAQ prompts useful in almost any narrative, designed to force the honesty the Earn Trust principle calls for rather than let a document only make its own best case:

- What were the biggest mistakes we made last period, and what did we learn?
- What are the key inputs for this business?
- What is the single biggest thing we can do to move the needle, and how will we organize to do it?
- What are the top reasons we should *not* do what we're proposing today?
- When push comes to shove, what won't we compromise on?
- What's hard about the problem we're trying to solve?
- If we had X more people or Y more dollars, how would we deploy them?
- What are the top three initiatives we've launched recently, and what did we learn?
- What dependencies do we have today that we wish we had control over?
